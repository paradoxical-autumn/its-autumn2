// why isn't your music on spotify?

spotify is an ecosystem designed to over-convenience you, undervalue music as a result, and keep you bound to its service. it is not a place for art, for authorship, for anything. it is a place where music is "content" and listeners are "product." it is an unsustainable nightmare that can only serve to erode the very thing it suggests it provides - music - though realistically, it is not concerned with providing that.

i am aware your phone might not have much storage on it, but surely you have a couple GB free? maybe cycle albums out each week, really get to know the stuff on your hard drive. hell, maybe have stuff on your hard drive at home at all, in a place where it can actually be there when you're counting on it. something you can back up and keep for a long while, not relying on it simply continuing to exist in the ether of streaming. if you don't have a couple GB free, maybe look into a cheap used MP3 player? or maybe a CD player, and you can burn discs? or maybe one of those CD-MP3 players, where you can burn discs full of hundreds of MP3s. lots of options.
